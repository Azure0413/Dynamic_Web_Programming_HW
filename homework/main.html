Vision

The Department of Transportation Management Science at National Cheng Kung University was founded in 1944, when it belonged to Taiwan Provincial Institute of Technology. In August 1963, the name of the department was changed from the Department of Traffic Management to the Department of Traffic Management Science. In August 1965, the Night Department was established. In August 1966, a master's program was added to the research institute. In 1977, a doctoral program was added. Graduate School of Management.

The original name of the department was the Department of Traffic Management, with Group A enrollment. In 1955, it was once changed to Group D. In 1963, the department was renamed "Department of Traffic Management Science" and resumed as Group A. In 1963, a new junior college system was established. After the implementation of the joint entrance examination, this series is the second category of departments.

Since 1986, in order to cooperate with the system of promoting the integration of departments and institutes, the master and doctoral programs of the department have been officially renamed as "Master and Doctoral Programs of the Department of Transportation Management Science". The training cultivates on-the-job master students.

Our Feature©

  • This department is the first professional transportation department with the longest history in Taiwan.
  • The Department is the only telecommunications management research institute in Taiwan.
  • The department covers the fields of highway, rail (high-speed railway, MRT, Taiwan Railway), maritime transportation, air transportation and telecommunications, and designs courses in four major fields: passenger transportation, logistics management, transportation technology and telecommunication management. Students can study across fields. With professional and complete teaching staff.
  • The department has close interaction with the industry, has substantial industry-university-construction-education cooperation, and provides abundant resources and scholarships.
  • Set up a professional research laboratory to assist in the formulation of industrial development and government transportation policies.
